# Document Draft Alignment Workflow

Interview type for aligning document drafts — written content, communications, proposals, documentation — with their intended purpose and audience.

---

## Domain-Specific Research (Phase 1)

### BLOCKING Research Targets
- **Audience:** Who reads this? What do they care about?
- **Style guides:** Existing organizational or industry style standards
- **Existing documents:** Similar documents for tone/structure reference
- **Communication context:** Where/how this will be distributed
- **Standards:** Compliance, formatting, or regulatory requirements

---

## Domain-Specific Challenge Angles (Phase 2)

- "Is this the right document for the goal? Would a different format serve better?"
- "Who is the primary reader, and what do they need from this?"
- "What's the one thing the reader should take away?"
- "Is the tone matched to the audience and context?"
- "What would make the reader stop reading halfway through?"

---

## Domain-Specific Questions (Phase 3)

### Purpose & Audience
- What action should the reader take after reading?
- What's the reader's starting knowledge level?
- Is this persuasive, informational, or instructional?
- What's the distribution channel (email, wiki, public, internal)?

### Structure & Tone
- What's the ideal length?
- What tone is appropriate (formal, conversational, technical)?
- Are there mandatory sections or compliance requirements?
- Should this follow an existing template?

### Content & Lifecycle
- What supporting evidence or data is needed?
- How often will this be updated?
- Who owns maintenance?
- What feedback/review process exists?

---

## Domain-Specific Output Additions

### Document Spec

```markdown
## Document Specification

**Type:** [Report / Proposal / Guide / Policy / Communication]
**Audience:** [Primary and secondary readers]
**Tone:** [Formal / Professional / Conversational / Technical]
**Length:** [Target word count or page count]
**Key Takeaway:** [One sentence the reader should remember]
```

### Style Notes

```markdown
## Style Notes

- Voice: [Active/Passive, Person]
- Terminology: [Domain terms to use/avoid]
- Formatting: [Headers, bullets, tables preferences]
- References: [Citation style, links policy]
```
