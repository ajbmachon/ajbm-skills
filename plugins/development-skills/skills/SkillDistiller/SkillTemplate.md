# Skill Template

Template for skills generated by SkillDistiller. The generated skill teaches **behavioral dispositions** — how an expert approaches a type of work — not checklists or rigid rules.

---

## Template

```markdown
---
name: {SkillName}
description: {Domain-specific description with USE WHEN triggers}
distilled_from: {session ID(s), file path(s), or "current session"}
distilled_date: {YYYY-MM-DD}
---
```

## Template Usage Notes

**Behavioral Dispositions section is the heart of the skill.** Everything else supports it.

- **Thinking Patterns** come primarily from **Analysis Modes** in the taxonomy
- **Attention Cues** come primarily from **Questions & Probes** in the taxonomy
- **Quality Checkpoints** come primarily from **Quality Gates** in the taxonomy
- **Anti-Patterns** come primarily from **Corrections** in the taxonomy

**Generalization level per section:**

| Section | Typical Level | Why |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Thinking Patterns | Most general | Cognitive frameworks transfer broadly |
| Attention Cues | Medium | Questions generalize but need context anchors |
| Quality Checkpoints | Medium-specific | Gates need concrete conditions to be actionable |
| Anti-Patterns | Most specific | Concrete examples are more useful than vague "don'ts" |

**The user controlled the generalization level during Step 3.** Respect their choices — don't re-generalize during generation.

**Size guidance:**
- Keep the generated skill under 500 lines
- If patterns are extensive, use progressive disclosure (reference docs for examples)
- Focus on the 5-10 most impactful dispositions, not exhaustive coverage
